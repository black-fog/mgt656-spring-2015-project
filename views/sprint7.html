For our second Google Analytics test, we conducted an experiment to see whether or not asking the user to donate or support would increase our conversion rate. This data was collected over 3 days and includes 110 experiment sessions.

Concluding Thoughts

Project Reflection

Going into the project, none of the team members knew each other. Our initial meeting allowed us to discover each other's strengths and we assigned roles accordingly. Jackson and William were assigned roles as the main coders and Heesun and Can were the product owner and scrum master. Each week, Heesun and Can changed their roles. Divvying up the roles this way allowed us to focus on parts that we feel comfortable with and also increased efficiency.

However, there were some initial communication issues within our team, especially during the first and second meetings. As mentioned above, we thought dividing roles would be efficient but in response, the communication response rate declined very dramatically to the point that project progress seemed to cease. Because of this, we were not able to accomplish desired number of stories in sprint 1 and 2. In sprint 2 we completed only one story. The main reason behind this was that we were not able to employ agile/scrum principles. However, we managed to arrange a meeting before the sprint report 3 and set up some rules that what we thought would align with agile/scrum principles. Two of the most important of them were: Emails should be replied to in 24 hours and if there is a technical issue, the coder would urgently notify product owner so that we could discuss and re-align stories.

With the help of those internal rules, our teamwork improved and it led to scrum meetings becoming more effective with each sprint. On day 1, we had little practice in knowing where to start, how to create a story, and how to assign story points. We markedly improved by the sprint report 3 write up and were able to proceed more efficiently. After this threshold, we knew how to better communicate in scrum meetings, such as how to transfer insight from product owner to coders and how to implement those stories.

Project Maintenance

Passing this project off to someone else would entail multiple actions. First, we would want to provide access to our GitHub repository, Heroku app, and our Waffle. Secondly, we would have a meeting where we detail what worked for us and what didn't, all while highly recommending the use of scrum/agile methodologies and open communication. We would also provide them with our Google Analytics data and encourage them to continue with testing so they can make a strategic decision based on a substantive amount of data. Finally, we would provide recommendations for additional features based on the grand vision we have for Black Fog, to be a better and improved version of Eventbrite.

Additional Features

In regards to additional features, we believe that there are a few ways to improve upon our site. Firstly, in addition to RSVPing for events, Black Fog could sell tickets to events for individual purchase. They could also expand the marketplace so individuals can re-sell their own tickets to events, similar to Ticketmaster. Another feature that could increase the utility of our site is to include a search bar. This would allow users to easily sift through different events. This would be even more useful if the user could search for events by date, location, and type of event, such as music, sports, etc. Another feature that could potentially increase Black Fog's brand is to include a sharing feature for events. This would allow users to publicize attendance of an event on social media channels like Facebook or Twitter, which would not only drive ticket sales but also increase awareness of Black Fog. Looking forward, after conquering the browser platform, we could also look forward to creating an app for iOS and Android devices.
