Progress

During our fourth sprint, we did not complete the list of stories in our product backlog. We were only able to create our about section, which contains our group members along with short bios. Last week our coders encountered some technical issues, which we discussed at one of our virtual standup meetings. They asked for more to plan out the event object before implementing it. Because of the time needed for the research, the product owner changed the sprint 4 structure and pushed two stories (above) into the sprint 4 backlog.

In terms of agile and scrum practices; as we mentioned in our previous report , we were able to deal with communication issues and in this sprint we realized the second positive outcome. After the discussion about the technical issue, we looked at our product backlog and realized that we had underestimated the complexity of create an event functionality. Also after a stakeholder’s new demand about event information, we added new story to our product backlog. We also deleted old completed stories from waffle. One can reach our updated backlog here. As of now, our application has an about page a skeleton new event page a home page that has our app logo and a list of events including the date and number of attendees. For sprint 5, we plan on continuing to work through our Product Backlog. We plan on completing items 1, 2, and 3 by our next sprint. We believe that with our improved communication and project processes in place, our coding and sprint reports will flow more smoothly with increased understanding by all team members.
